# Ohio Legal, Insurance, And Licensing Checklist

This is an operational checklist, not legal, tax, or insurance advice. Confirm with an Ohio attorney, accountant, insurance broker, and each local jurisdiction or venue.

## Entity And Tax Setup

| Status | Task | Why It Matters | Source / Where To Confirm |
|---|---|---|---|
| Not started | Form Ohio LLC or choose entity structure | Separates business operations and supports bank/insurance setup | Ohio Secretary of State business services: https://www.ohiosos.gov/businesses/ |
| Not started | Get EIN | Needed for bank account, tax accounts, payroll later | IRS EIN assistant: https://www.irs.gov/businesses/small-businesses-self-employed/get-an-employer-identification-number |
| Not started | Open business bank account | Keeps personal and business use separate | Bank/accountant |
| Not started | Set bookkeeping categories | Needed for depreciation, equipment, personal use, mileage, fuel, software, insurance | Accountant |
| Not started | Confirm Ohio sales tax/vendor license obligations | Entertainment, rentals, concessions, and services can have different treatment | Ohio Department of Taxation: https://tax.ohio.gov/business |
| Not started | Track personal vs business use | The trailer doubles as personal asset; usage log protects deductions | Accountant |

## Insurance

| Status | Coverage | Why It Matters | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Not started | Commercial general liability | Venues often require COI and additional insured status | Quote before first paid event |
| Not started | Commercial auto / towing exposure | Personal auto policy may not cover business towing | Broker must understand trailer events |
| Not started | Inland marine / equipment coverage | Covers launch monitor, projector, consoles, TVs, generator, tools | Use actual equipment values |
| Not started | Trailer physical damage | Protects the trailer itself | May be separate from auto |
| Not started | Abuse/molestation or participant liability question | If minors are present, ask broker about exclusions | Especially for kids' parties |
| Not started | Workers comp | Needed when hiring hosts/employees | Check Ohio BWC when staffing |

## Towing, Weight, And DOT

| Status | Task | Why It Matters | Source / Where To Confirm |
|---|---|---|---|
| Not started | Confirm tow vehicle GCWR/GVWR and hitch ratings | Avoid unsafe or illegal towing | Vehicle door sticker/manual, trailer VIN plate |
| Not started | Confirm whether CDL rules apply | Heavy gooseneck combinations can trigger stricter licensing | Ohio BMV CDL info: https://www.bmv.ohio.gov/ |
| Not started | Stay below thresholds if possible | Common CDL trigger is combination GCWR 26,001 lb or more with trailer over 10,000 lb GVWR, but confirm Ohio specifics | Ohio BMV, FMCSA |
| Not started | Confirm registration/commercial plate needs | Business trailer use may affect registration | Ohio BMV |
| Not started | Maintain pre-trip checklist | Protects safety and insurance position | Internal SOP |

## Movie Public-Performance License Question

| Status | Task | Why It Matters | Source / Where To Confirm |
|---|---|---|---|
| Not started | Decide if movie mode is private-home-only, public, or paid event | The licensing need depends on audience, setting, admission/payment, and rights | Attorney + licensing provider |
| Not started | Contact MPLC | MPLC provides public performance licensing for many non-theatrical uses | https://www.mplc.org |
| Not started | Contact Swank / Movie Licensing USA | Swank handles many public performance and title-specific licenses | https://www.swank.com |
| Not started | Do not rely on Netflix/Disney+/consumer streaming for paid/public screenings | Consumer streaming typically does not grant public performance rights | Platform terms + attorney |
| Not started | Add movie-license clause to booking agreement | Prevents customer misunderstanding and protects package limits | Attorney |
| Not started | Price movie licensing into package margins | The business plan included a movie public performance license line item | Budget model |

## Booking Agreement Clauses To Draft

- Deposit and final payment timing.
- Cancellation and rescheduling.
- Weather, surface, access, and parking requirements.
- Generator/power and noise rules.
- Damage to equipment, controllers, screens, trailer, and seating.
- Supervision and minor participation expectations.
- Liability waiver and assumption of risk.
- Movie-title/licensing responsibility and limits.
- Travel fee and service radius.
- Photo/video permission for marketing.

## Priority Before First Paid Event

1. Entity/bank/tax path confirmed.
2. Insurance bound and COI available.
3. Trailer and towing legality confirmed.
4. Booking agreement and waiver reviewed.
5. Movie licensing resolved or movie mode limited.
6. Safety equipment installed: extinguisher, CO detector, first-aid kit, cable management, secure mounts, generator clearance.
