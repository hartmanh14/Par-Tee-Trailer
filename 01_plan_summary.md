# Mobile Entertainment Business - Alignment Summary

Assumptions for this launch pack:
- Chosen tier: Lean, approximately $45,000 startup budget.
- Funding placeholder: $20,000 owner cash plus approximately $25,000 equipment/trailer financing or dealer financing.
- Timeline target: operational within 6 months, with first 90 days focused on Validate to Build.
- Operator model: owner-operator for year one, with contract event help added only after repeatable SOPs and booking volume.
- Market: Troy, Ohio as home base, serving roughly a 45-minute Troy/Dayton/Miami Valley radius.

1. The business is a climate-controlled mobile entertainment trailer that converts between golf simulator, private movie theater, and multi-console gaming lounge.
2. The core advantage is one mobile asset with three revenue modes, which should fill more of the calendar than a single-purpose game truck, golf simulator, or outdoor movie rental.
3. The plan's best first move is validation before capital spend: map local competitors, gather 15-20 buyer conversations, and secure 3-5 soft commitments.
4. The trailer should be an enclosed bumper-pull or gooseneck unit, not a self-powered truck, because a tow vehicle failure does not ground the whole entertainment asset.
5. For the Lean tier, the build likely depends on a used 24-foot enclosed trailer, SkyTrak-class launch monitor, shared projector/screen infrastructure, modular seating, and controlled DIY/vendor retrofit.
6. Target customers split into private events, corporate/team events, and recurring institutional accounts such as schools, churches, HOAs, breweries, and fundraisers.
7. Starting price architecture should mirror the plan: gaming $350-$500, movie $300-$600, golf $400-$700, combo $700-$1,200, corporate half-day $1,500-$3,500.
8. Break-even is most sensitive to average booking price and bookings per week. Owner-operator labor materially improves payback because the plan's mid-tier opex included $18,000 in part-time host labor.
9. Long-lead and launch-gating items are trailer acquisition, launch monitor/simulator hardware, insurance, vinyl wrap, and movie public-performance licensing.
10. Main risks are seasonality, equipment failure, licensing exposure, liability, high startup cost, and owner dependence. The mitigation pattern is start lean, document SOPs early, insure properly, and use the three-mode model to stabilize demand.
