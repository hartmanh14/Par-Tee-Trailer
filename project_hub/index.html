Buying and retrofitting the trailer

Open vendor emails
1

Define hard constraints

  • Max cash at risk and monthly payment comfort.
  • Tow vehicle make, model, year, GCWR, GVWR, hitch, payload.
  • Storage location, security, shore power availability.
  • Launch deadline and minimum viable equipment package.
2

Quote the trailer

  • Start with Midwest Trailer Factory in Troy.
  • Compare at least two marketplace/dealer alternatives.
  • Require interior height, ramp dimensions, empty weight, GVWR, payload, axle rating, and lead time.
  • Do not place a nonrefundable deposit until swing clearance and towing math pass.
3

Prove golf layout

  • Confirm ball-to-screen distance, player-to-wall clearance, ceiling height, and launch monitor spacing.
  • Mock the group lounge seating and the removable fundraiser desk.
  • Decide where clubs, cables, projector, PC, HVAC, and generator live in transit.
  • Verify emergency exit and guest flow before finishes.
4

Power, climate, and safety

  • Estimate loads for A/C, heat, projector, launch monitor, PC, lights, fans, and chargers.
  • Choose shore power, generator, or hybrid plan with safe cable routing.
  • Add fire extinguisher, CO detector, first-aid kit, ventilation plan, and tie-downs.
  • Have electrical work reviewed by qualified help before paid events.
5

Install simulator system

  • Quote SkyTrak/SkyTrak+ and Uneekor-class options for mobile commercial use.
  • Pick hitting mat, impact screen, projector, enclosure/netting, PC/tablet, and software.
  • Test 30-minute sessions, driver swings, missed shots, and repeated setup/teardown.
  • Document calibration steps in the operating SOP.
6

Make it bookable

  • Bind insurance and get COI process ready.
  • Draft booking agreement, waiver, cancellation, damage, and weather terms.
  • Finish website lead capture, scheduler, deposit flow, and quote templates.
  • Run two free/discounted demos before paid launch.

Risk controls

Rules before real money goes out

No trailer deposituntil tow capacity, swing clearance, payload, insurance quote range, and financing path are confirmed.
No paid eventuntil liability insurance is bound and booking terms are ready.
No public movie offerunless Phase 2 is reactivated and public-performance licensing is resolved.
No generator setupwithout CO/fire plan, ventilation, cable routing, and venue permission.
No school/park eventwithout venue approval, COI needs, parking plan, and supervision expectations.
No optimistic payback claimsuntil conservative/ramp cases are added to the workbook and validated by real demand.
